## The Multiverse Inspector (several books out)

**Author idea (2026-06-25), parked for a few books:** At some point the Shop **reconnects with the full multiverse network**. Not long after, **an inspector shows up**, complaining that his *not-called-a-Tardis* kept breaking down and **wouldn't restart until dragged to the next universe over**.

**Why parked:** depends on the Shop's reconnection arc, which is far downstream. Hold until the series reaches the reconnection beat.

**Hooks already in place (worth knowing):**

- [Inference] This rhymes with established Book-1 folklore. In `04_Worldbuilding.md`, the **local building inspector's truck "died every time it pulled up to the property and only restarted after being towed past the town line"** (appears in the *Arthur's Curios* setting entry, the *Grovesend* / *Arthur's Curios (Culture)* folklore, and *Arthur's Disappearance*). The same "vehicles won't run near the Shop" motif scales perfectly from a small-town pickup to a cosmic inspector's malfunctioning craft — the mundane gag can be quiet foreshadowing of the cosmic one.
- [Speculation] Ties to the Shop's cosmology (`07_Secrets.md` → *The Shop's true purpose*): a node "encapsulating chaos" that has been **offline/decayed through neglect** would plausibly be *off the network*, so reconnection → a bureaucratic visit (compliance, re-registration, an overdue inspection) is a natural comedic + plot consequence. The inspector is the multiverse's equivalent of the building inspector the Shop has been stonewalling for years.

**Tone target:** comedic-bureaucratic. The cosmic equivalent of a zoning officer, defeated by the same property that defeats the local one.
