## Session 1 — 2026-06-24 · "The long build day"

A single long session that stood the project up from a braindump to a complete Chapter 1 plus most of the Story Bible. Reconstructed order below is approximate.

### Foundations & operating rules

- **The LitRPG seed.** The core hook: an inherited curio shop that rewards Stepan with **credit for stewardship** (cleaning, small repairs) — an XP-like progression loop **with no combat required**. Logged as genre-fresh: most LitRPG earns power through danger; Stepan earns it through care of an inherited space, which suits a near-70 protagonist with a bad hip.
- **The five Hard Rules were established:**
  1. The fairy princess is **only ever "Prince" or "Cat"** in prose/dialogue/summaries/AI continuations — never "Princess," "Aya," "Stronghome," etc. In-world: exposure makes her transformation permanent. Drafting: the secret is the engine of her tension.
  2. **Stepan begins with zero awareness** that magic is real or that he has any potential. Interior monologue reaches for mundane explanations first; the bloodline framing arrives late.
  3. **Label unverified content** (`[Inference]`/`[Unverified]`/`[Speculation]`); never present generated content as fact.
  4. **Don't paraphrase or reinterpret** the author's input; write stated canon into the relevant file.
  5. **Author draft prose (root `.docx`) is private** unless explicitly opened.

### Drafting conventions locked

- **Working title:** *Inventory of Impossible Things*.
- **POV / tense:** third-person limited, **present tense**, anchored to Stepan in Ch. 1 (converted from the draft's original past tense).
- **Voice:** reflective, sardonic; push for a touch more humour with a sarcastic edge — sharpen, don't flood.
- **File workflow:** prose lives in `Chapters/` (master `Chapter_NN.md`, with a generated `.docx`). Mid-session the author designated **the `.docx` as the reference version**; Claude switched to editing the `.docx` and regenerating the `.md` from it.

### The System Spec (08) — first lock

The progression system was locked as a **single two-track economy**: **XP** (→ guardian level, gates abilities) + **Store Credit** (spendable balance). Difficulty-weighted earning with no repetition decay; balance floors at zero (no debt, never stolen). Disclosure was **Ledger-only** (no HUD). The **cane/staff** sub-system was specced as a bloodline-keyed capacitor (passive charge → zaps, cap rises across the series). Pacing flags raised: a single series-long curve with **no level caps** and a restorative effect that can **de-age** Stepan — removing two usual guardrails, leaving "the Shop withholds on purpose" as the primary brake. *(This single-economy model was superseded the next day — see Session 2.)*

- **Cast created** (`03`): Stepan Novacek, Danica Lapczuk, Arthur's Curios (the Shop), Prince (the Cat), The Ledger.

## Session 2 — 2026-06-25 · "The systems day"

Today's session turned the teed-up idea into locked, detailed canon, then reframed the Shop itself.

### Magic XP curve — proposed, analyzed, locked

- The author proposed an **exponential curve**: 10 XP → L1, doubling thereafter (cumulative to level *n* = 10 × 2^(n-1)).
- Claude analyzed it and **compared to AD&D**: the curve mirrors AD&D's *early-game* geometric shape, but AD&D switches to **arithmetic** growth at "name level," whereas this doubles forever (far steeper up high). Flagged that **AD&D characteristics don't rise with level** — stat-increases-on-level-up is a video-game/D&D-3e+ convention, not AD&D. Math verified programmatically.
- **Locked:** Magic XP doubles **forever, no tail cap** (the deliberate brake on godlike power — each level costs as much as the whole career to date). **XP-per-cast scales with spell level**, so high levels are only reachable by casting high-level magic. Each level grants **+1 characteristic point + one spell.**

### Shop SP curve — locked

- **1 SP → L1**, doubling until the per-level cost hits **1,024 (=2^10) at L11**, then **flat 1,024/level** thereafter — the classic AD&D geometric-then-arithmetic shape, so stewardship stays reachable while raw magic chokes itself. **Store Credit folded into Shop SP.**

### Characteristic cap

- All stats cap at **18** except **Strength**, which continues in `18/+NN` notation to a ceiling of **`18/+99`** (homage to old-D&D exceptional Strength). Allocation is an authorial lever. `[Inference]` +99 is effectively unreachable — a series-spanning aspiration.

### Spell acquisition — two phases

- **Pre-exposure:** no choice — Stepan is simply *granted* **Magic Missile at L1** (a door-breaker, throttled in-scene by Prince's magic resistance, so it reads as a harmless "zap the cat" beat while the cane quietly becomes deadly).
- **Exposure beat (~L3, tentative):** a section of the curios **glows** and offers a **floating spell-book / HUD** of selectable books, including a **"Learn About Your Magical Experience"** primer.
- **Drafting seed planted:** after 3–4 L1 zaps Prince shrugs off, the cat **dodges** and the missile **cracks a door/wall** — the reveal that the spell (and the cane) are genuinely dangerous.

### Disclosure surfaces & sequencing

- **Two surfaces, kept separate:** Shop SP appears **in the Ledger**; Magic XP appears **outside** it (silent pre-exposure, then the glowing spell-book). The magic interface stays **dormant** until Stepan suspects magic is real (Hard Rule 2).

### Earning rules — locked numbers

- **Minor fix = 1 SP, capped 10 SP/day** (the early-game brake); **notable jobs pay a lump** (windows inside + out = 10 SP); **first-of-a-kind pays big** (first sale), routine repeats = 0 unless notable; **first entry to a newly unlocked room** pays once; **defending the Shop** spills a little SP onto a magic act (rat killed with Magic Missile = 5 XP **and** 2 SP) — kept small and controlled; **spiderwebs with spiders = 2 XP** (micro-defense, silent pre-exposure).
- **Steward stat gains:** **+1 Charisma at L5 and L7** (presence/authority); optional Constitution floated, not locked.
- **§7 workman rule:** a hired non-relative earns nothing but credits **Stepan a fraction** of the SP; a **blood relative earns SP in their own right** — seeding multi-guardian / a possible **second bloodline** (Danica, far-future).

### The Shop as a second progression body

- **Two separate numbers:** Stepan's **Steward level** vs the **Shop's own level**. The Shop is **sentient enough to direct its own growth** and may **manifest upgrades unbidden** (a snack-bar at Shop L6) as well as on Stepan's direction.
- **Gentle, bounded curve** (proposed, to tune): never the millions of the Magic curve, so the *building* visibly transforms across Book 1 while the *man* is still low-level.

### The Shop's cosmology — new canon (author-only)

- The Shop is **ancient but decayed through neglect**, and holds **a place in the multiverse: defending order by *encapsulating* chaos rather than eliminating it. It is all about balance.** A containment vessel, not a weapon; the curio "junk" is partly a containment array; stewardship is cosmic load-bearing work. Recorded as a **reader-secret** in `07`, with a Lore stub in `04` and a cross-ref in `06`. `[Inference]` This leans the Shop's origin-of-sentience toward "bound/bestowed" over "accreted," though origin remains unchosen.

### Parked for the far future
