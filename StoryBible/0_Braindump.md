# Braindump

Stepan is old (nearing 70), grumpy, and hindered by a bad hip.  As a child, his uncle Arthur used to tell him fantastic stories of other worlds and creatures and magic, stories that turned Stepan into a lifelong lover of fantasy stories.

Arthur disappeared 7 years earlier.  He was out of touch for most of the last 40 years, except for the odd, long letter that often read like a fantasy author trying out a new plot.  With his disappearance, he has been declared dead, and his will bequeaths a curio store to Stepan.  Stepan takes a train from Toronto to Ingersoll, where he meets up with a scheduled ride to Grovesend and this strange inheritance.

The store is sentient in some form, and it recognizes Stepan's bloodline, treating him as a true owner.  This means hot, fresh, and good coffee, a stocked fridge and larder, and some amenities.  It also means that Stepan earns credit for cleaning the very dusty store and for making small repairs.

There is a tomcat in residence.  It's actually not a tom but rather an ensorcelled fairy princess who is not at all happy with her situation.  However, she knows that if her identity is ever discovered, she'll remain a tom until she dies.  She discovers that she can talk with Stepan telepathically and proceeds to complain and snark with heavy sarcasm and attitude.  Enough so that Stepan names the cat Prince

The story starts on the train ride.  Arriving in Ingersoll, Stepan embarrasses himself by overlooking Danica while searching for Dan but this is quickly remedied.  The ride to Grovesend gives Danica a chance to describe the town and the shop.

As events unfold, various incidents lead Stepan to realize that magic might be real.  It starts with the telepathic cat.  Although the magic nature isn't noted right away, the fact that the cane is now a magic staff (disguised as a cane) and as it powers up, it can give light but painful zaps to Prince.
