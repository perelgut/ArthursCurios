## The custom sentient-object stat scheme (canonical)

Adopted by the author 2026-06-24 as the standard stat block for sentient objects, replacing the AD&D 3–18 scale (which fit objects awkwardly). These four axes are now canon for every sentient object; the AD&D six-stat block is deprecated for objects.

Four axes, each rated 1–10:

- **Influence** — how strongly the object can act on the physical/mental world (Shop bends rooms; Book only marks pages).
- **Memory** — depth and span of what it holds (centuries vs. a single guardianship).
- **Reach** — how far its awareness and effect extend from its physical body (whole building vs. arm's length of the counter).
- **Will** — how much it pushes its own agenda vs. passively records.

Canonical values:

| Object | Influence | Memory | Reach | Will |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arthur's Curios (Shop) | 8 | 9 | 7 | 6 |
| The Ledger | 2 | 10 | 3 | 7 |

The contrast the scheme captures: the Shop is high-Influence / low-Will (enormous power, but it holds back on principle), while the Ledger is low-Influence / high-Will (almost no power, but a strong opinion about how things should go).

## Arthur's Curios — the Shop

**Canonical profile:** [`03_Characters.md` → Arthur's Curios](03_Characters.md) (role, AD&D stats, personality, motivations, arc, physical description).
**Related:** [The Bloodline](04_Worldbuilding.md) • Stepan Novacek • The Ledger • Prince (Cat) • Arthur (former guardian).

### Purpose and cosmology `CANON` (author, 2026-06-25)
The Shop is **ancient but decayed through neglect.** Its deeper nature: it holds **a place in the multiverse, defending order by *encapsulating* chaos rather than eliminating it** — a containment vessel, not a weapon. **Its whole principle is balance.** This reframes the curio collection: the "junk" includes chaotic/dangerous things the Shop *contains* to keep the larger order intact, and a guardian's real job is maintaining that containment, not just dusting.

> **Reader-secret.** This cosmic purpose is **author-only** and unknown to Stepan early (Hard Rule 2). The full statement lives in [`07_Secrets.md` → The Shop's true purpose](07_Secrets.md); keep it out of prose, summaries, and AI continuations until the reveal.

### Origin of sentience
`[Unverified]` Not yet established in the source material. Open question for the author. Candidate framings to choose among:

- `[Speculation]` Accreted: the building became aware slowly, the way "very old things are patient" (its own canon temperament), waking over generations of guardianship rather than being switched on.
- `[Speculation]` Bestowed: an early bloodline guardian (pre-Arthur) deliberately seated something into the structure.
- `[Speculation]` Bound: a separate entity is housed *in* the building rather than the building itself being alive.

`[Inference]` The balance/containment purpose above leans toward the **bound** or **bestowed** framings — a structure built or seated to *hold* something tends to imply an act of making, not mere accretion — but the author has not chosen. Whichever is chosen has series stakes — see the `04_Worldbuilding.md` open question about what happens to the Shop, the Ledger, and Prince if Stepan dies without a recognized heir.

### Self-directed growth `CANON`
The Shop levels on its **own progression track**, separate from Stepan's Steward level and far gentler (bounded, never the millions of the Magic curve — see [`08_System_Spec.md` §3](08_System_Spec.md)). It is "sentient enough to direct its own growth": at level milestones it can **manifest upgrades unbidden** (the snack-bar at Shop L6) as readily as it executes changes Stepan directs. This is the mechanical expression of its canon "does not ask permission" temperament.

### Manifesting transient objects `CANON`
Beyond standing provision and permanent upgrades, the Shop **conjures objects on demand and withdraws them once their purpose is served.** Example (Ch. 2): a **toaster** appears on the kitchen counter when Stepan wants toast, then is gone — with the bread sealed away — by the time he returns. `[Inference]` This is distinct from the **self-tidying** of permanent kitchenware: transient objects arrive *because they are needed* and leave when they are not, which is why Stepan keeps catching the room rearranged and blames his own inattention. A reusable deniable-magic device for future scenes (the larder, the right tool turning up on the hall table, etc.).

### Decline without a Keeper `CANON` (Ch0)
The Shop is **ancient and weakening**, and a guardianless stretch accelerates the slide: with no bloodline Keeper to feed its strength, its self-maintenance frays (intermittent lighting, a **stuttering coffee monolith**, un-swept mess, a fading Ledger), and the old stories warn it can eventually "eat its own memory" and fold in on itself — shrinking to a single box or bottle to await the next finder. `[Canon, Ch0]` The building has outlived **at least two prior bloodlines** and keeps "the ghost of every Keeper who ever failed to keep." A new bloodline guardian (Stepan) is what lets it **rebuild** — which is exactly what the Restoration track (`08`) measures. (This is why the monolith etc. are perfect again by Ch1: the Keeper has arrived.)

### Food as language `CANON` (with the cat)
Through the vacancy, the Shop's main channel to the cat is **food quality as carrot-and-stick**: kibble (plus cold water, a chilled floor) signals displeasure or a test; fresh **sushi-grade salmon** (or a single cube of turkey as a concession) signals approval or apology. Exchanges run as ritual — the cat refusing first offers on principle, the Shop rolling the tin across the floor as a last gambit. This is the food-specific dialect of the Shop's broader "warmth = provision, displeasure = inconvenience" voice.

### Reach and limits
Drawn from canon in `03`: acts on the physical world "within and immediately around its walls"; can "slow or redirect time in subtle ways"; extends a low-level restorative effect on those it accepts; cannot fully self-repair, only "create the conditions for repair"; communication is entirely nonverbal and easily misread.

`[Inference]` Practical drafting consequence: the Shop can never solve a plot problem that exists outside its threshold. Anything happening in Grovesend proper, on Danica's roads, or beyond is off its board. That's a useful built-in limiter on a very powerful entity.

### Voice guide (per craft-advice item 4)
The Shop has no words, so its "voice" is its **environmental vocabulary**. Lock these defaults so its "speech" stays recognizable across scenes:

- **Register:** parental but unsentimental; comfort delivered as logistics, not affection (hot coffee, stocked larder, eased hip).
- **Grammar:** warmth = provision; displeasure = "structural inconvenience" (sticking drawers, a floorboard that gives at the wrong moment, a draft).
- **Timing:** "impeccable and occasionally pointed" — its single sharpest tool is *when* something happens, not what.
- **Refuses to discuss / cannot:** it does not explain itself, does not ask permission, does not apologize. It will let Stepan stub his toe on a board it could have fixed.
- **Pass-the-test tell:** if a gesture could be read as either coincidence or intent, and a careful character notices the *pattern* rather than the single event, it's the Shop talking.

### What it knows that Stepan doesn't
`[Inference]` It recognizes the bloodline (canon) — so it knows, from the threshold, what Stepan is, while he reaches for mundane explanations (Hard Rule 2). The dramatic irony is that the Shop is being a careful host *because* of something Stepan hasn't learned about himself yet.

## The Ledger

**Canonical profile:** [`03_Characters.md` → The Ledger](03_Characters.md) (role, AD&D stats, personality, motivations, arc, physical description). Canonical name is **The Ledger**; "The Book" and "Stepan's Book" are aliases.
**Related:** [The Bloodline](04_Worldbuilding.md) • Stepan Novacek • Arthur's Curios • Arthur (former guardian, holds his final unsurfaced entries).

### Origin of sentience
`[Unverified]` Not established in source material. `[Inference]` Canon already implies a mechanism worth formalizing: Stepan's entries appear in "a deep, slightly iridescent black ink that was not present in the book before his arrival," and the Book "recognized Arthur as a bearer." So the Book's sentience reads as **bloodline-keyed** — it activates/responds for a recognized guardian. Candidate question for the author: is the Book aware *between* guardians (dormant but conscious), or does it effectively sleep until a bearer arrives?

### Reach and limits
From canon in `03`: it "cannot act, only record"; "cannot answer direct questions, only offer what it chooses to surface and when"; records inside-the-shop events "with great precision but has limited awareness of events beyond the threshold." It holds "an unbroken record of every guardian, every significant object, and every consequential event," including Arthur's final, not-yet-surfaced entries.

`[Inference]` So the Book is the inverse of the Shop: near-zero physical Influence, but the deepest Memory in the building. Its only lever is **selective disclosure** — *which* entry surfaces, and *when*. That's its entire power set, and it's a good one for a mystery.

**Records the whole situation `CANON` (2026-06-26).** The Ledger logs *every party to a consequential event*, not only the guardian — goblins, the breach, the Shop weakening and healing, and other people present, **including non-bloodline ones** (e.g. Danica when she manifests at the Book 1 finale). It is the Shop's *record*, separate from the Shop's *service*: writing someone into the account of a night is **not** the same as crediting them SP/XP (see `08` §4/§7). Hence the finale image of the Book "scratching away for both of them" even though the Shop is attuned only to Stepan's line. `[Canon, 2026-06-26]` The Ledger holds entries about **Danica** (the returning cleric), **surfaced at the Ch10 finale** when Stepan shows her the book — e.g. dryly noting that *it is about time a cleric returned and took up some responsibilities.* The dry-aside voice makes the gut-punch land.

### Voice guide (per craft-advice item 4)
This object *does* use words (written entries only — never spoken), so the voice guide is literal:

- **Vocabulary:** precise, economical, bureaucratic formality.
- **Sentence length:** short, clinical; the dryness is the joke.
- **Signature move:** a neutral fact followed by a parenthetical aside that is "technically neutral but unmistakably pointed."
- **Hard rules of style (canon):** never uses exclamation points; uses italics "sparingly and therefore devastatingly"; favors understatement when alarm is warranted, clinical specificity when vagueness would be kinder.
- **Refuses to discuss:** anything it isn't ready to surface — especially Arthur's final entries. Its constraint is "the gap between what it knows and what it is permitted to say directly."
- **Pass-the-test tell:** if you can cover the speaker's name and still know it's the Book because the line is a flat fact with a quietly murderous parenthetical, the voice is holding.

### Sample entry format `[Speculation]`
A proposed house style for how Book entries render in prose — author to accept or adjust:

> *Guardian applied beeswax to the east shelving, 14:20. Third attempt. (The shelving had not requested it.)*

`[Inference]` Establishing one fixed visual/typographic convention for Book entries early supports craft-advice item 5 (lock your status-window/system-information style in the first 5–10k words) — the Ledger's entries are effectively this story's "status window."
